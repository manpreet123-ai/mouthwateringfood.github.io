Pindi Chole Without Onion Garlic

Prep Time: 20 mins
Resting Toime: 10 mins

Pindi chole without onion garlic is a traditional chole recipe which uses pomegranate and amchur in the masala. This makes this Pindi chole different from other chole recipes. The unique feature of this recipe is that here the chole acquires a dark black color and is not very fluidy. These chole are super delicious and yummy and delectable. You are going to love them. Read along to know how to make homemade no onion garlic pindi chole.

For Begginers
How to make Pindi Chole Without Onion Garlic:
(1)Soak the chole in 6 cups of water overnight or for atleast 8 hours till it becomes double in size.
(2)Throw away the water in which the chole were soaked.
(3)In a pressure cooker add the strained chole, baking powder, tea leaves, dried amla and the whole spices – bay leaf, brown elaichi, cloves and salt.
(4)Pressure cook the chole in water. The quantity of water should be double the quantity of chole. Close the lid and wait till you hear two whistles. This will ensure that there is a bite in the chole and not completely pulpy. If you are not using baking powder then you will have to cook for 6 – 7 whistles depending upon the size of your chole. Then let the pressure release naturally.
(5)Dry roast all the spices separately on a dry tawa till you can smell the aromas and the masalas change color. The anardana should be nearly black. Let it cool and then
(6)grind them into a powder.
(7)Once the chole are done, separate the water from chole. Keep this water aside for later use.
(8)Let the chole cool for some time.
(9)Then add red chilli powder, ajwain, jeera powder, kasoori methi, amchur powder, dhaniya powder, black salt (kala namak), roasted grinded masala prepared earlier and 1/4 tsp hing.
(10)Mix all together with a light hand. Cover and keep aside for 1/2 hr – 1 hr.
(11)Now in a pan add three tablespoons of oil/ghee. Fry ginger juliennes till crisp and golden. Now add the slit green chillies. Fry for a min.
(12Pour this tadka over the chole and mix well.)
(13)Now add tamarind pulp. Stir carefully on high heat to avoid breaking the chole.
(14)Add 1 – 2tblsp of water kept aside from boiled chole. This will allow the masalas to get cooked and coat the chole properly.
(15)After 4 – 5 mins, switch off the flame.
(16)Serve garnished with the ginger strips and green chillies.
(17)Also serve Nimbu halves and sliced onions with it
(18)Tastes best with Bhatura/Kulcha/puri, even lachcha paratha. And if eating with rice, make it a bit wetter.
(19)To make it more tasty, give a tadka of 1 tblsp ghee and 1 tsp red kashmiri chilli powder on top before serving.
